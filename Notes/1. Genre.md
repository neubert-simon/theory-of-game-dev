# Game Genres

## Conceptual Clarification
> Genres categorize video games based on the following criteria:
- Gameplay interaction
- Gameplay challenges (rules, goals, obstacles...)

Different from book genres, game genres **don't** factor in visuals, narratives, setting or game worlds.

To classify games, the following characteristics should be defined:
<b>
- Technological characteristics
- Gameplay characteristics
</b>

### First Person Shooters

#### Gameplay Characteristics
- First person perspective
- 3D environment (often hyper-realistic)
- Combat is the primary design element
- Combat is mostly gun and projectile weapon-based
- Wide range of powerful hand-held weaponry
- World traversal
    * Historically: Slow on-foot roaming in corridor-based worlds
    * Today: Usable vehicles like cars, boats and planes, large outdoor environments
- Multiplayer gaming is often an essential part
    * Death-match, Capture the flag, etc.
    * Very common in eSports competitions
- Implicit depiction of violence (often not youth-free)
- Occasionally simple puzzles (e.g. lever mechanics or key cards)

#### Technological Characteristics
- Extremely high (hardware) demands and very challenging to build
- Many technological innovations arose from FPS games
- Efficient rendering of large 3D virtual worlds
    * Highly optimized and carefully tuned rendering engine!
        - Space partitioning
        - Portal-based rendering
        - Various culling mechanisms
        - Very responsive camera and aiming controls
- High fidelity animations of arms and guns (reload, jamming, ...)
- High fidelity animations of non player characters
- High fidelity artificial intelligence of non player characters
- Forgiving player character motion and collision detection
- Small-scale online multiplayer capabilities (typically up to 64 players)

### Platformers / Third-Person Games

#### Gameplay Characteristics
- Core gameplay element: Jumping
- High demands on player dexterity
    * Many movement options, which may require precise execution
- Moving platforms, ladders, ropes, trellises and other locomotion modes
- Puzzle-like environmental elements
- Third-person “follow-camera”
    * Stays focused on the avatar
    * Rotation is typically controlled by the human player
- Often coupled with other genres

#### Technological Characteristics
- Comparable to First Person Shooters
    * Also extremely high (hardware) demands and very challenging to build
- A great deal of emphasis is placed on the main character
    * Rich set of abilities and locomotion modes
    * High-fidelity full-body animations, with complex blending
- Extended character collision system (compared to FPS), e.g.
    * Standing on narrow platforms
    * Grabbing onto ropes, ladders
    * Avoiding enemies and projectiles
- Complex camera mechanics
    * “Intelligent” camera that stays focused on the action
    * Collision system to avoid clipping through foreground and background geometry

### Racing Games

#### Gameplay Characteristics
- Primary task: Driving a vehicle on some kind of race track
- Players have to master the vehicle's controls and find the optimal racing line
- Corridor-based or looped tracks, sometimes with short-cuts
- Very fast travel speed (compared to on-foot roaming)
- First- or Third-person camera
- Many sub-genres
    * Simulation
    * Arcade-Racer
    * Street-Racer
- Different types of competition
    * Time-based (who is the fastest)
    * Versus Races
    * Shoot at one another / collecting loot / other modes
- Players often have the opportunity to tune and customize their vehicles

#### Technological Characteristics
- Very detailed vehicle graphics and animation
    * High polygon counts and high-resolution textures
    * Damage and contamination model
- Complex physics simulation
    * Friction, centrifugal force, bounce, crashes, wind, slipstream, etc.
- Rendering of long race tracks
    * Level of detail (usually multiple levels)
    * Partitioning into sectors for optimization purposes
- Rendering / visibility determination / path finding / etc.
- Weather effects like rain, snow or sandstorms
    * Affecting visual appearance and driving physics
- Complex artificial intelligence system for non-player opponents
    * One of the toughest challenges in the fields of game AI!
- Sophisticated camera collision detection

### Fighting Games

#### Gameplay Characteristics
- Two humanoid characters (fist-) fighting each other
    * They usually have equal physical strength (in PvP)
    * Mostly focused on close range combat
- Many attack options
    * Strong / weak, arms / legs, grabs, special moves, etc.
- Complex control schemes
    * Players have to master techniques like blocking, counter-attacking and combos
    * Specific button combinations execute special attacks
- Fights last over several rounds
- Very restricted roaming limited to some sort of ring or arena
    * The sub-genre “Brawler” or “Beat'em up” allows more character navigation
- Relatively static camera, focused on the action
    * Might zoom in/out or showcase special moves

#### Technological Characteristics
- Very detailed characters
    * High-definition models
- High polygon counts
- High-resolution textures
- Skin shaders with subsurface scattering, sweat effects
- Damage and injury model
- High-fidelity animations
- Physics-based cloth and hair simulation
- Accurate hit detection
    * Head, Feet, Arms, Torso, …
- User input system capable of handling complex button combinations
- Crowds, but otherwise relatively static backgrounds
    * No need for culling or world subdivision
    * No need to stream resources at runtime
- Often fixed frame rate
    * Gameplay is often tied to the frame rate (e.g. windup or counter frames)

### Role Playing Games (RPG)
#### Gameplay Characteristics
- The player controls the actions of a protagonist who lives immersed in the fictional world
- Originated in pen-and-paper role-playing games
    * Much of the same terminology, settings and game mechanics
    * Electronic medium removes the necessity for a gamemaster
- Highly developed story and setting, divided into a number of quests
    * Large emphasis on story-telling and narrative elements
    * World exploration (story is often mapped onto exploration)
- Items and Inventory
    * Finding loot and collecting it, such as clothing, weapons, and armor
    * Trading and management aspects
- Experience and levels
    * Character's are gaining new skills, weapons, and magic (positive-feedback cycle)
- Complex and challenging combat
    * Real-time or turn-based

#### Technical Characteristics
- Extremely large game worlds
    * Subdivision into multiple areas (possibly connected by portals)
    * Excessive use of level of detail (long viewing distance)
- Complex character and combat logic
    * Large set of attributes and dependencies
    * Mathematical equations often determine the outcome of a battle
    * Balancing of countless probabilities
    * Necessity for a very good random number generator
- Extensive dialogue system
    * Individual decisions and nested conversation options
    * Large number of text lines and speech samples
- Sophisticated character AI for party members and enemies
- Challenging to save game progress (infinite number of states)
    * Serialization of the whole game world

### Real-Time Strategy (RTS) Games

#### Gameplay Characteristics
- Real-Time: does not progress incrementally in turns
- Typically war games
    * Commanding an army and overwhelming the enemy
- The Player only has indirect control of units
    * “Clicking and dragging” to select units
    * Giving orders and commands
    * Tactical decisions
- Players have the ability to build new units and structures
- Resource gathering and investment
    * Resources are usually scarce and hence limit construction and research
- Research and development
- Fixed viewing angle (or very limited)
- Multiplayer gaming is often an essential part
    * Very common in eSports competitions

#### Technical Characteristics
- Each unit is relatively low-res so that large numbers can be rendered
- Variable quantity of units must be supported
- High demands on artificial intelligence
    * Steering behaviors and path finding for multiple units (avoidance!)
    * Tactical decisions and strategic planning
- Grid-based world
    * Proper alignment of units and buildings
    * Optimized rendering and AI
- Input features borrowed from desktop applications
    * Mouse input (click, drag, buttons, etc.)
    * Demanding and complex user interfaces
- Orthographic rendering
    * Modern RTS games use perspective projection and a true 3D world

### Massively Multiplayer Online Games

#### Gameplay Characteristics
- Defined as any game that supports huge numbers of simultaneous players, usually all playing in one very large, persistent virtual world.
- Sub-genres
    * MMORPG (Role Playing Game)
    * MMORTS (Real Time Strategy)
    * MMOFPS (First Person Shooter)
- Gameplay experience is usually very similar to the respective small-scale multiplayer counterparts.
- Strong social aspects and extensive inter-player in-game communication
    * Inter-user chat or voice-over-IP services
    * Quests often require players to work together
    * Various external communication platforms
    * Highly addictive (symptoms comparable to nicotine or alcohol addiction)
- Individual financing models
    * Regular subscription fees
    * Free-to-play elements

#### Technical Characteristics
- At the heart of every MMOG is a battery of powerful servers.
    * Maintaining the authoritative state of the game world
    * User sign-in and sign-out management
    * Providing communication services
    * Payment system (billing and micro-transactions)
    * Statistics and data evaluation
    * User database
- Graphics fidelity is almost always lower than in its non-massively multiplayer counterparts.
    * Huge world sizes
    * Large numbers of supported users
    * Deliberately keeping hardware requirements low
- Character editor and other customization options
    * Models are made of multiple components (head, hair, torso, etc.)
    * Modifiable scripts and data files (e.g. Lua or XML)
