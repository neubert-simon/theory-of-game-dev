## Basic Law (Grundgesetz)
**Article 5, Par. 1**
>There shall be no censorship.

**Article 5, Par. 2**
> These rights shall find their limits […] in provisions for the protection of young persons […].
➡️ German Children and Young Persons Protection Act (JuSchG)

## Children and Young Persons Protection Act (JuSchG)
**Article 12 Paragraph 1**
> Games, shall not be accessible in the public for Children […] unless they have been cleared and labeled for the respective age group […].

Retail sales of unrated games is illegal in Germany!
➡️ USK rating required

**Article 14 Paragraph 2**
> The following release codes may be issued for […] play programs by […] an organization of voluntary self-control in a procedure on the basis of Sub-Clause 6.
1. "Freigegeben ohne Altersbeschränkung",
2. "Freigegeben ab sechs Jahren",
3. "Freigegeben ab zwölf Jahren",
4. "Freigegeben ab sechzehn Jahren",
5. "Keine Jugendfreigabe".


**Article 14 Paragraph 6**
> Common procedure for release and labeling of […] play programs based on findings recorded from reviews […].

This function is carried out by USK.

**Article 28 Paragraph 3**
> A regulatory offense is committed by a person […] who encourages a Child to assume behaviors that ought to be prevented.

Sales of unsuitably rated games to minors makes the seller personally liable criminally.



## Index

###  Index List A
- May not be supplied to children and young persons
- May not be publicly advertised, displayed or broadcast
- May not be distributed via mail order
- Must remain legally available to adults
    * Special shops to which only adults have access
    * Via the Internet in a restricted user group for adults only

### Index List B
Further restrictions in addition to list A.
- Relevant under criminal law
- Potential for confiscation

### Seized by court order (aka confiscated)
- May not be sold, even to adults

## Unterhaltungssoftware Selbstkontrolle (USK)
> Every game needs to be played almost 100% through to be rated.

The US in contrast only requires a questionnaire.

### Advisory council
There is an advisory council which advises the USK on rating criteria and procedures.
It is made up of social workers, business figures, church members, and regular people.

| Rating     | Typical content                                                                                                                                            |
| ---------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **USK 0**  | Suitable for all ages. No frightening scenes, no realistic violence, no sexual content, very mild conflict only.                                           |
| **USK 6**  | Mild cartoon or fantasy violence, slightly tense or scary moments, but nothing likely to distress young children.                                          |
| **USK 12** | Moderate violence, some frightening scenes, mild sexual references or language. Violence is not overly graphic or realistic.                               |
| **USK 16** | More realistic or frequent violence, stronger language, more mature themes (crime, sexuality, drugs), but not excessively graphic.                         |
| **USK 18** | Intended for adults only. May contain graphic violence, explicit language, strong horror, sexual content, or other mature themes that could impair minors. |
